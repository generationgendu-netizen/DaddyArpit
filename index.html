Document Transparency

Know exactly what paper
you should receive.

Most people in India don't know what documents to ask for. We list every document and whether it's legally required — so you're never left unprotected.

📄

Rental Agreement

A written contract between you and the owner. Must include duration, price, fuel terms, and damage policy. Always get this signed.

✅ LEGALLY REQUIRED
🛡️

Vehicle Insurance Copy

Confirms the vehicle is insured. Ask for this — if the vehicle meets with an accident and it's uninsured, you could face liability.

✅ ALWAYS DEMAND THIS
📋

RC (Registration Certificate)

Proof the vehicle is legally registered. If you're caught without it, you may be fined. Ask for a clear photocopy.

✅ MUST CARRY
⚖️

FIR Liability Clause

Specifies who files the FIR in case of theft or accident. Without this clause, you may be held fully responsible for vehicle theft.

✅ ASK EXPLICITLY
🔧

Damage & Wear Policy

Defines what counts as "damage" vs normal wear. Avoid shops that keep your entire deposit for a minor scratch.

⚠️ RECOMMENDED
💰

Security Deposit Receipt

Always get a written receipt when paying a security deposit. This is your proof for getting it back. Shops are legally bound to return it.

✅ GET THIS IN WRITING
⛽

Fuel Terms Document

Clearly states the fuel level at pickup and what level you must return it at. Prevents fuel theft disputes.

⚠️ RECOMMENDED
📸

Pre-Rental Condition Photos

Take photos or video of the vehicle before driving — especially dents, scratches, tyres. RentEase prompts you to do this on pickup.

⚠️ DO THIS ALWAYS
⚠️

What to do if a shop refuses to give any documents

This is a red flag. Do not hand over your ID as "collateral" (this is illegal in India). Walk away from any shop that refuses to give a rental agreement. All verified shops on RentEase are contractually required to provide at minimum: rental agreement, insurance copy, and RC xerox.
